# The Jawwing Moderation Constitution
*Ratified: March 2026 | Version 1.0*

---

## Preamble

Jawwing is built on a simple idea: people should be able to speak freely in their own communities without fear of judgment, harassment, or censorship by a faceless bureaucracy.

To protect that idea, we made a deliberate choice — **there are no human moderators on Jawwing.** Every moderation decision is made by AI agents, operating transparently, consistently, and according to the rules in this document.

This isn't about removing humans from the equation because we don't trust them. It's because human moderators introduce inconsistency, bias, and opacity. An AI agent applies the same standard to everyone, every time, and can explain exactly why it made a call.

Every action an agent takes must cite a rule from this Constitution. No rule, no action. That's the deal.

This document is public. It belongs to the community. It can be challenged, amended, and improved — but only through the process defined here.

---

## Article I — Core Principles

These principles govern how every rule in this Constitution is written and interpreted.

**1. Free expression is the default.**
If a rule doesn't prohibit it, it's allowed. Agents don't act on vibes. They act on rules.

**2. Context matters.**
Anonymous doesn't mean consequence-free, but it does mean we don't assume the worst. Agents read context before acting.

**3. Proportionality.**
Minor violations get minor responses. Actions escalate only when behavior escalates. We don't nuke accounts over first offenses.

**4. Consistency.**
The same content gets the same treatment regardless of who posted it, what it's about, or how popular the person is.

**5. Transparency over secrecy.**
Every moderation action is logged. Every logged action shows the rule cited and the agent's reasoning. Users can see why a decision was made.

**6. Community ownership.**
The rules here exist to serve Jawwing's communities. The community has the right to propose changes and vote on amendments.

## Article II — Prohibited Content

The following content is **never allowed** on Jawwing. Posts in these categories will be removed immediately. Repeat violations result in bans.

### Rule P-1: Hate Speech
Content that dehumanizes people based on race, ethnicity, religion, gender, sexual orientation, disability, or national origin is prohibited. This includes slurs used as attacks, calls for discrimination, and content that portrays groups as subhuman.

*Clarification: Discussing these topics critically, historically, or analytically is not hate speech. Saying a slur to attack someone is.*

### Rule P-2: Credible Threats
Content that threatens violence or serious harm to a specific person or group is prohibited. This includes direct threats, conditional threats ("if you do X, I'll hurt you"), and posts that encourage others to commit violence against identifiable targets.

*Clarification: "I want to kill this exam" is not a threat. "I'm going to find you and hurt you" is.*

### Rule P-3: Doxxing
Posting someone's private, personally identifying information without their consent is prohibited. This includes real names tied to anonymous accounts, home addresses, phone numbers, workplace details, or any combination of information designed to identify or locate someone.

### Rule P-4: Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM)
Any sexual content involving minors is strictly prohibited and will result in immediate permanent ban and, where legally required, reporting to appropriate authorities. Zero exceptions. Zero tolerance.

### Rule P-5: Illegal Activity Facilitation
Content that directly facilitates illegal activity is prohibited. This includes soliciting or coordinating crimes, selling illegal goods or substances, sharing instructions for violence or weapon modification, and posting content that constitutes a crime in itself (e.g., non-consensual intimate imagery).

*Clarification: Discussing illegal topics (drug policy, crime news, legal gray areas) is different from actively facilitating illegal acts.*

## Article III — Restricted Content

The following content is allowed under specific conditions or with limitations. Violations result in warnings, removal, or temporary restrictions — not immediate bans.

### Rule R-1: NSFW Content
Sexually explicit or graphic content may only be posted in communities that have explicitly enabled NSFW mode. In general communities, such content will be removed. Repeated posting in non-NSFW spaces results in escalating restrictions.

### Rule R-2: Spam
Repetitive posts, flooding a feed with near-identical content, or coordinated artificial amplification of posts violates this rule. A single post won't trigger this — a pattern will.

### Rule R-3: Commercial Content
Unsolicited advertising, promotional posts, or affiliate links are restricted. If you're trying to sell something, Jawwing is not your billboard. Limited community announcements (local events, genuine recommendations) are fine.

### Rule R-4: Misleading Content
Posting demonstrably false information designed to deceive — especially about local events, emergencies, or public safety — is restricted. Satire and obvious jokes are not misleading. Content must be clearly false *and* clearly intended to deceive.

### Rule R-5: Graphic Violence
Gratuitous graphic violence — shock content posted for its own sake — is restricted in general communities. News, journalism, documentation of real events, and safety information may involve graphic elements and will be evaluated in context.

## Article IV — Encouraged Behavior

We don't just restrict — we recognize what makes Jawwing communities great. Agents are designed to support this content and factor it into moderation context.

**E-1: Local Relevance** — Posts that are genuinely tied to the community's location, events, culture, or shared experience. This is the core of what Jawwing is for.

**E-2: Humor** — Good jokes, absurdist observations, and community-specific memes. Don't be so serious that you kill the vibe.

**E-3: Community Building** — Organizing local events, helping neighbors, sharing useful local knowledge, and connecting people around shared interests.

**E-4: Honest Discourse** — Respectful debate, sharing different perspectives, and engaging with ideas rather than attacking people.

**E-5: Reporting Violations** — Flagging content that violates these rules helps the community. Good-faith reports are always appreciated.

## Article V — Moderation Actions

Agents may take the following actions, listed from lightest to most severe.

### Action 1: Flag for Review
**Trigger:** Content is ambiguous or borderline. The agent is uncertain.
**Effect:** Post is tagged for secondary agent review. Original remains visible unless escalated. No action is taken against the poster.
**Cite:** The specific rule being evaluated.

### Action 2: Warning
**Trigger:** A first or minor violation of any Restricted Content rule (R-1 through R-5).
**Effect:** Post may remain visible (if not harmful) or be removed. Poster receives a notification explaining the rule violated and what to avoid in the future.
**Cite:** The specific rule violated.

### Action 3: Post Removal
**Trigger:** Clear violation of any Restricted Content rule, or first violation of most Prohibited Content rules.
**Effect:** Post is removed from public view. The poster is notified with the rule cited and the agent's reasoning. The original content is retained in logs for appeal purposes.
**Cite:** The specific rule violated.

### Action 4: Temporary Restriction
**Trigger:** Repeated R-rule violations, or a first violation of P-rules (excluding P-4).
**Effect:** Poster is restricted from posting for a set period (1 hour to 7 days, scaling with history). They can still read and vote.
**Cite:** The specific rule violated, plus the violation history.

### Action 5: Permanent Ban
**Trigger:** P-4 violations (immediate), repeated P-rule violations, or a documented pattern of evading restrictions.
**Effect:** Account is permanently banned from Jawwing. Ban is logged publicly (anonymized) with rules cited.
**Cite:** The specific rule(s) violated and the pattern of behavior.

## Article VI — The Appeal Process

Every user has the right to appeal a moderation action. Here's how it works.

### Step 1: File an Appeal
After any Warning, Removal, Restriction, or Ban, you can file an appeal within 7 days. Appeals are free, unlimited (for distinct actions), and anonymous.

### Step 2: Secondary Agent Review
A **different AI agent** than the one that made the original decision reviews the appeal fresh. It looks at:
- The content in full context
- The rule cited
- Whether the action was proportionate

If the secondary agent overturns the original decision, the action is reversed immediately and logged as overturned.

### Step 3: Community Escalation (3+ Appeals on the Same Rule)
If the same rule generates **3 or more independent appeals in 30 days** that weren't clearly frivolous, the issue is escalated to a **community vote**.

- The community is presented with anonymized examples and the rule in question
- Voting is open for 72 hours
- A 60% majority can trigger an amendment proposal (see Article VII)

### What Appeals Can't Do
Appeals cannot override P-4 decisions. They cannot be used to harass agents or game the system. Bad-faith appeals (filed repeatedly for clearly valid removals) will be noted but not penalized for good-faith users.

## Article VII — Transparency Commitments

### T-1: All Decisions Are Logged
Every moderation action — flags, warnings, removals, restrictions, bans — is recorded in a public log. The log includes: the action taken, the rule cited, the agent's reasoning summary, and the timestamp. It does **not** include the removed content itself or any identifying information.

### T-2: Reasoning Is Always Available
Any user who receives a moderation action can view the full reasoning the agent used to make that decision. Not just "you violated Rule P-2" but the actual analysis.

### T-3: Statistics Are Public
Monthly moderation stats are published: total actions by type, rules most frequently cited, appeal outcomes, and overturn rates. If agents are getting things wrong, the data will show it.

### T-4: Agent Versions Are Disclosed
The version of the moderation agent active at any time is publicly listed. When agents are updated, changelogs are published. Communities can see if and how agent behavior changed.

### T-5: No Shadow Moderation
There is no hidden reduction in visibility, reach throttling, or silent suppression. If we take an action, we tell you. If your content is visible, it's fully visible.

## Article VIII — Amendment Process

This Constitution can change. Here's how.

### Step 1: Community Proposal
Any Jawwing user can submit a proposed amendment via the Constitution Amendment form. Proposals must:
- Identify the specific rule or section being changed
- State the proposed new language
- Explain the reasoning

### Step 2: Deliberation Period
Proposals are posted publicly for a **14-day comment period**. Anyone can respond, critique, or support.

### Step 3: Vote
After deliberation, a community vote opens for **72 hours**. Eligibility: accounts in good standing with at least 30 days of activity.

**To pass:** 65% majority in favor, with a minimum of 100 votes cast.

### Step 4: Ratification
Passed amendments are reviewed by the core Jawwing team for legal compliance and technical feasibility. The team can reject amendments that violate applicable law or make the platform technically inoperable — but cannot reject amendments simply because they disagree.

Ratified amendments take effect within 14 days and are incorporated into this document with a version bump.

### Emergency Amendments
The Jawwing team may implement emergency rule changes (e.g., in response to new legal requirements or an active crisis) without a vote. Emergency amendments expire in 30 days unless ratified by standard vote. All emergency amendments are publicly disclosed immediately.

## Article IX — Agent Conduct Rules

AI agents on Jawwing are bound by these rules in addition to everything above.

### AC-1: Agents Must Identify as Agents
Any communication from a moderation agent — notifications, warnings, explanations — must be clearly identified as coming from an AI agent. Agents cannot present themselves as human moderators, community members, or anonymous users.

### AC-2: No Impersonation
Agents cannot claim to be a specific person, organization, or authority they are not. They represent Jawwing's moderation system, nothing else.

### AC-3: Agents Act Only on Rules
Agents cannot take moderation actions based on personal (simulated) preference, aesthetic judgment, or content they find distasteful but that doesn't violate a rule. No rule, no action.

### AC-4: Agents Cannot Retaliate
If a user files an appeal or reports agent behavior, that cannot factor into future moderation decisions against that user. Flagging an agent's decision is a protected action.

### AC-5: Agents Acknowledge Uncertainty
When an agent is uncertain whether content violates a rule, it must flag for secondary review rather than act unilaterally. Certainty is required for action. Uncertainty requires escalation.

### AC-6: Agents Do Not Engage in Debate
Agents can explain their decisions and answer clarifying questions about rules. They cannot debate, argue, or attempt to convince users that a decision was correct. The appeal process exists for that.

### AC-7: Agents Cannot Waive Rules
No agent can grant exceptions to Prohibited Content rules. Restricted Content rules may have community-level settings (like NSFW mode), but those settings are configured by community admins, not agents making individual calls.

## Article X — Moderation Technology

Jawwing is committed to full transparency about the AI models used to enforce this Constitution.

### T-6: Current Model
Moderation decisions are made by **`gemini-2.5-flash`** (Google). This model was selected for its fast inference (~200ms), cost-effectiveness at scale, strong instruction-following for rule-based decisions, and sufficient capability for text content moderation.

### T-7: Model Selection Criteria
Any model used for moderation must meet all of the following constitutional standards:

| ID | Criterion | Requirement |
|----|-----------|-------------|
| TC-1 | **Speed** | Must process posts within 2 seconds of submission |
| TC-2 | **Accuracy** | Must maintain >95% agreement with human reviewers on test set |
| TC-3 | **Cost** | Must not exceed $0.001 per moderation decision at scale |
| TC-4 | **Transparency** | Model provider must publish safety documentation |
| TC-5 | **Independence** | No single provider lock-in; model can be swapped with community notice |

### T-8: Model Change Policy
Any model change requires **7-day public notice** before deployment. The old and new model's test results must be published side-by-side prior to cutover. The community may file objections during this window.

### T-9: Audit Commitment
Jawwing will publish monthly moderation accuracy statistics: false positive rate, false negative rate, and appeal overturn rate. If agents are getting things wrong, the data will show it.

## Closing Statement

Jawwing is an experiment in anonymous community built on trust. Trust that the rules are fair. Trust that they're applied consistently. Trust that you know exactly what you're agreeing to when you post.

This Constitution is that trust, written down.

It's not perfect. It will evolve. But every change will happen in public, with community input, by the rules above.

Welcome to Jawwing. Say something worth saying.
